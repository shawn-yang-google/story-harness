# Archetypes, Not Stereotypes

## The Archetypal Story
Unearths a **universally human experience**, then wraps itself inside a **unique, culture-specific expression**. Creates settings and characters so rare that our eyes feast on every detail, while illuminating conflicts so true to humankind that it journeys from culture to culture.

## The Stereotypical Story
Reverses this pattern — confines itself to a **narrow, culture-specific experience** and dresses in **stale, nonspecific generalities**. Suffers a poverty of both content and form.

## The Double-Edged Encounter
Master storytellers deliver two pleasures:
1. **Discovery of a world we do not know** — an exotic, cliché-free zone where the ordinary becomes extraordinary
2. **Finding ourselves** — deep within alien characters and conflicts, we discover our own humanity

> "Stereotypical stories stay at home, archetypal stories travel."

## Example
LIKE WATER FOR CHOCOLATE — mother and daughter clash over universal conflicts (dependence vs independence, permanence vs change), yet wrapped in never-before-seen detail of a specific Mexican world.
