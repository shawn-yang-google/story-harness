# Principles, Not Rules

A **rule** says, "You must do it this way." A **principle** says, "This works... and has through all remembered time."

The difference is crucial. Your work needn't be modeled after the "well-made" play; rather, it must be well made within the principles that shape our art.

## The Three Writer Types
- **Anxious, inexperienced writers** obey rules
- **Rebellious, unschooled writers** break rules
- **Artists** master the form

Mastering the form means understanding *why* principles work, not memorizing *what* rules dictate. This understanding gives freedom to create within proven patterns while finding fresh expression.
