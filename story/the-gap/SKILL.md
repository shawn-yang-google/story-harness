# The Gap

The "clay" of storytelling — the **substance** from which all stories are shaped.

## Definition
The Gap is the rift that opens between what a character **expects to happen** when they take action and what **actually happens** as a result.

## How It Works
1. Character assesses their situation
2. Character takes action based on **expectation** of a certain response
3. The world reacts in a way that is **more powerful or different** than expected
4. A **gap** cracks open between expectation and result
5. Character must take a **new, more difficult action**

## Why It Matters
- The Gap is the **source of conflict** — the world resists the character's will
- It creates **surprise** — the audience experiences the unexpected with the character
- When the gap opens for the character, it opens for the **audience**, creating the "spark of life" that moves the heart

## Writing from the [ANIMATED_FILM]
To create emotional truth, the writer must **inhabit the protagonist's point of view** and experience the gap subjectively. Feel the character's expectation, then the shock of the unexpected result.

## The Core Truth
> The substance of story is the gap that opens between what a human being expects and what they get.
