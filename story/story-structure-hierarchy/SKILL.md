# Story Structure Hierarchy

The five levels of story design, from smallest to largest:

## Beat
An exchange of **behavior in action/reaction**. Beat by beat, changing behaviors shape the turning of a scene. The smallest element of structure.

## Scene
An action through **conflict in continuous time and space** that turns the value-charged condition of a character's life with perceptible significance. Ideally, every scene is a **Story Event**.

## Sequence
A series of scenes (generally **two to five**) that culminates with **greater impact** than any previous scene. Title each sequence to clarify its story purpose.

## Act
A series of sequences that peaks in a **climactic scene** causing a **major reversal of values** — more powerful than any previous sequence or scene.

## Story
A series of acts building to a **last act climax** that brings about **absolute and irreversible change**. The great sweep of change from opening condition to end condition is the arc of the film.

## The Building Principle
Make the smallest element do its job and the deep purpose of the telling will be served. Let every phrase of dialogue or line of description either turn behavior/action or set up conditions for change.

> Beats build scenes, scenes build sequences, sequences build acts, acts build story to its climax.
