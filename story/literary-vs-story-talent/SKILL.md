# Literary Talent vs Story Talent

Two singular and essential talents for writing — with **no necessary connection** between them.

## Literary Talent
The creative conversion of **ordinary language into a higher, more expressive form**, vividly describing the world and capturing its human voices.
- **Common** — in every literate community, hundreds can write beautifully
- Material: **words**

## Story Talent
The creative conversion of **life itself to a more powerful, clearer, more meaningful experience**. Seeks out the inscape of our days and reshapes it into a telling that enriches life.
- **Rare** — pure instinctive genius may produce quality once, but not consistently
- Material: **life itself**

## Key Insight
Stories do not need to be **written** to be told. Theatre, film, opera, mime, poetry, dance are all forms of the story ritual. The most powerful moments on screen require no verbal description, no dialogue — they are **image, pure and silent**.

## The Hierarchy
Story talent is **primary**, literary talent secondary but essential. This principle is absolute in film/television and truer for stage/page than most writers wish to admit.
