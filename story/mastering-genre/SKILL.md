# Mastering Genre

## The Imperative
Master your genre. Research it thoroughly — **watch and read dozens of works** within it. Know its history, conventions, and audience expectations before attempting to transcend them.

## Creativity = Mastery, Not Ignorance
Genre conventions provide creative limitations that actually **inspire originality**. Working within and against conventions forces the writer to find fresh approaches to familiar material.

## The Process
1. **Identify** your genre and its sub-genre
2. **Study** the canon — the best examples, the worst, and everything between
3. **Map** the conventions — required events, character roles, settings, values
4. **Internalize** audience expectations
5. **Create** from deep knowledge — then you earn the right to subvert

## The Love Test
> Be honest in your choice of genre, for of all the reasons for wanting to write, the only one that nurtures us through time is love of the work itself.

Don't write something because intellectual friends think it's important. Don't write for critical praise. Write the kind of story you'd stand in line in the rain to see.
