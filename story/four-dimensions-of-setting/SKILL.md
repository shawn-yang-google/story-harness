# Four Dimensions of Setting

Every story's setting is defined by four dimensions that shape narrative possibilities:

## 1. Period
The story's **place in time** — contemporary, historical, future, or fantasy. Determines the social norms, technology, language, and possibilities available to characters.

## 2. Duration
The story's **length through time** — the span within the characters' lives that the telling covers. May be minutes (UNITED 93) or decades (FORREST GUMP).

## 3. Location
The story's **place in space** — specific geography, buildings, rooms, landscapes. The physical world the characters inhabit.

## 4. Level of Conflict
The story's position on the **hierarchy of human struggles**:
- **Inner Conflict**: Mind, body, emotions within the character
- **[CLASSIC_FILM]l Conflict**: Family, friends, lovers
- **Social/Institutional Conflict**: School, career, church, justice system
- **Environmental Conflict**: Physical world — dangerous streets, disease, time running out

Stories may operate at **any combination** of these levels.
