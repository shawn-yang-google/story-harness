# Scene Turning Values

## The Test
Look at each scene and ask:
1. What **value** is at stake in my character's life at this moment?
2. How is that value charged at the **top** of the scene? (positive/negative)
3. Where is this value at the **close** of the scene?
4. **If the answer is the same** — why is this scene in my script?

## The Principle
> No scene that doesn't turn.

If the value-charged condition stays unchanged, nothing meaningful happens. The scene has activity — talking, doing — but nothing changes in value. It is a **nonevent**.

## Why Non-Events Exist
Almost certainly for **exposition** — to convey information to the eavesdropping audience. If exposition is a scene's sole justification, a disciplined writer will trash it and weave its information elsewhere.

## The Universal Rule
- **Action genres** turn on public values: freedom/slavery, justice/injustice
- **Education genres** turn on interior values: self-awareness/self-deception
- Regardless of genre: **if a scene is not a true event, cut it**

## Example
[CHARACTER_NAME_A] and [CHARACTER_NAME_B] arguing from bedroom → kitchen → garage → highway is one scene (not four). The four locations are camera setups. The scene turns only when [CHARACTER_NAME_B] slams the door and says "It's over!" — taking the couple from love/together to hate/apart.
