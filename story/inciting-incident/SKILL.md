# The Inciting Incident

## Definition
The **first major event** of the telling — the primary cause for all that follows. It radically upsets the balance of forces in the protagonist's life, turning their world upside down for better or worse.

## What It Must Do
1. **Radically upset** the protagonist's balance of life
2. **Arouse conscious desire** — the protagonist wants to restore balance
3. May also arouse **unconscious desire** that contradicts the conscious one
4. **Launch a quest** through escalating conflict
5. **Promise a climax** — create the dramatic question the story must answer

## Timing
The inciting incident must happen within the **first quarter** of the telling. It can:
- **Open the story** (JAWS, STAR WARS) — immediate disruption
- **Arrive after setup** — establish the protagonist's world before disrupting it

Delayed inciting incidents risk losing the audience.

## Main Plot vs Subplots
Main plot and subplots each have their own inciting incidents. Subplot inciting incidents should **echo, contradict, or complicate** the main plot's inciting incident.

## The Question It Raises
Every inciting incident raises a **dramatic question** that the audience expects the story to answer. This question drives the entire narrative forward to its climax.
