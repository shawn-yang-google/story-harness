# Meaning Through Climax

## The Principle
The controlling idea is **not** stated in dialogue, narration, or authorial commentary. It is expressed through the **story's climax**.

> What happens at the end IS the meaning.

## How It Works
The last act climax is the storyteller's **ultimate commentary** on the value at the core of the story. The resolution of the central conflict — which side of the value wins — communicates the writer's vision of how life works.

## Didacticism vs Aesthetic Meaning

### Didactic (Weak)
The writer drives the narrative to **prove an idea**, reducing characters to mouthpieces. Meaning is **stated** rather than **expressed**. The audience feels manipulated.

### Aesthetic (Strong)
Meaning emerges **naturally from the story's structure**, expressed through dramatized conflict. The audience **discovers** the meaning through experience rather than being told it.

## The Test
If you can remove the climax and still state the story's theme, the meaning was told rather than shown. The climax should be the **only place** where the full meaning becomes clear — inseparable from the structural resolution.
