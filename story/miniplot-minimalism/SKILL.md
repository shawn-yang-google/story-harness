# Miniplot — Minimalism

Minimalism begins with elements of Classical Design but **reduces** them — shrinking, compressing, trimming, or truncating the prominent features of the Archplot.

## Key Reductions
| Element | Miniplot Variation |
|---------|-------------------|
| Ending | Open — some questions unanswered |
| Conflict | Emphasis on internal |
| Protagonist | Multiple or passive |
| Change | Subtle, interior |

## Important Distinction
Miniplot does **not** mean "no plot." Its story must be as beautifully executed as an Archplot. Minimalism strives for **simplicity and economy** while retaining enough of the classical to still satisfy the audience.

## The Multiplot Variation
Splinters the film into several subplot-sized stories, each with a separate protagonist. Softens the roller-coaster dynamic of Archplot. Examples: PARENTHOOD (6 protagonists), SHORT CUTS, PULP FICTION.

## Examples
TENDER MERCIES, PARIS TEXAS, FIVE EASY PIECES, PELLE THE CONQUEROR, A RIVER RUNS THROUGH IT, TO LIVE.
