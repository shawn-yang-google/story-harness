# The Obligatory Scene

## Definition
The **obligatory scene** is the climax of the last act — the scene the audience knows must come and expects to see. It answers the **dramatic question** raised by the inciting incident.

## The Promise
Every inciting incident **promises** a specific climactic confrontation:
- If a murder launches the story → the obligatory scene is the confrontation with the killer
- If lovers are torn apart → the obligatory scene resolves whether they reunite
- If a quest begins → the obligatory scene is the final test

## Why It's Obligatory
The audience has been **waiting for this scene** from the moment the inciting incident disrupted the protagonist's life. To deny them this scene is to **cheat** them of the experience the story promised.

## Requirements
1. It must **answer** the central dramatic question
2. It must create **absolute, irreversible change**
3. It must be the **most powerful scene** in the story
4. It must feel both **surprising and inevitable**

## The Connection
> Inciting Incident → Story Spine → Obligatory Scene

These three elements form the structural backbone. The inciting incident creates the question, the spine sustains the pursuit, and the obligatory scene delivers the answer.
