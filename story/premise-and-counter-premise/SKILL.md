# Premise and Counter-Premise

## The Dialectical Process
A story argues its controlling idea through a dialectic:

### Premise
The **positive vision** expressed through the story — what the writer wants to be true about the value at stake.

### Counter-Premise
The **opposing negative argument** — the forces, events, and characters that challenge the premise.

## How It Works
1. Story events **alternate** between premise and counter-premise
2. Scenes that support the positive vision are followed by scenes that challenge it
3. The argument **escalates** — each round of premise/counter-premise is more intense
4. The **climax tips the balance** one way, expressing the final meaning

## Why Both Sides Matter
- A story that only argues the premise becomes **propaganda**
- A story that only argues the counter-premise becomes **nihilism**
- The strongest stories give **equal weight** to both sides, making the climactic resolution feel earned and truthful

## The Writer's Honesty
The writer must be willing to argue **against** their own beliefs through the counter-premise. Only by honestly dramatizing the opposition can the final meaning achieve genuine power.
