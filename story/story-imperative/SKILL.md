# The Story Imperative

The sign on the door reads **"Story Department"** — not "Dialogue Department" or "Description Department."

## The Hierarchy
- A **good story** makes a good film possible
- **Failure to make the story work** virtually guarantees disaster
- The better the storytelling, the more vivid the images, the sharper the dialogue
- Lack of progression, false motivation, redundant characters, empty subtext — these are root causes of bland text

## The Labor
Of the total creative effort in a finished work, **75 percent or more** goes into designing story:
- Who are these characters?
- What do they want? Why?
- How do they go about getting it?
- What stops them?
- What are the consequences?

## Literary Talent Is Not Enough
> If you cannot tell a story, all those beautiful images and subtleties of dialogue waste the paper they're written on.

Countless writers lavish dressy dialogue on anorexic yarns and wonder why scripts never see production. Others with modest literary talent but **great storytelling power** watch their dreams living on screen.

Story demands both vivid imagination and powerful analytic thought. Designing story tests the maturity, insight, and knowledge of the writer.
