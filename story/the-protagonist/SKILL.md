# The Protagonist

## Definition
A protagonist is a **willful character** whose strength of will (quality and quantity) drives the story through conflict to meaningful change.

## Conscious Desire
Every protagonist has a **known, conscious goal** — a specific, tangible objective they pursue throughout the story. This desire is the engine of the plot.

## Unconscious Desire
Multi-dimensional protagonists often have a **self-contradictory unconscious desire** that conflicts with their conscious goal. This creates **inner conflict** — the character is working against themselves without knowing it.

## Example
A protagonist consciously desires career success but unconsciously fears the intimacy that success would enable. The story dramatizes the collision between these desires.

## Willpower
The protagonist's willpower must be strong enough to sustain the story through escalating conflict. This doesn't mean the protagonist always acts — even a passive protagonist must have the inner strength of will to endure.

## Key Principle
The protagonist's desire — conscious and unconscious — creates the **spine** of the story. Every event relates back to this desire, either advancing or obstructing it.
