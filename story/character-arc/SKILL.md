# Character Arc

## Definition
The finest writing not only **reveals** true character but **arcs or changes** that inner nature, for better or worse, over the course of the telling.

## The Arc Structure
- **Beginning**: The character presents a mask (characterization) — who they appear to be
- **Middle**: Pressure and conflict strip the mask, revealing deeper nature
- **End**: The character has been fundamentally changed — who they truly become

## Direction of Change
- **Positive arc**: Character grows, gains wisdom, becomes better (THE ACCIDENTAL TOURIST)
- **Negative arc**: Character deteriorates, corrupts, falls (THE GODFATHER)
- **Ironic arc**: Change is ambiguous — simultaneously better and worse

## The Relationship to Structure
Character arc is **inseparable from story structure**. The events of the story create the pressure that forces change. The change in character IS the meaning of the story.

## Key Insight
Not all stories require character arcs — some stories (particularly action-driven Archplots) reveal character without changing it. But the deepest, most resonant stories **transform** their protagonists.
