# The Principle of Creative Limitation

**Limitation is vital for creativity.** A well-told story takes place within a **small, knowable world**.

## The Paradox
- The **larger** the world → the more **superficial** the writer's knowledge → the more **clichéd** the result
- The **smaller** the world → the more **complete** the knowledge → the more **original** the story

## How It Works
Setting **boundaries** forces the writer to dig deeper into the world's internal logic, leading to original choices rather than clichéd ones. When you limit the world, you must mine it deeply — and depth produces the unique details that make stories feel authentic and surprising.

## Practical Application
Choose the most **restrictive setting** that still allows your story to be told. Then excavate that setting until you know it as thoroughly as the characters who live in it. The constraint isn't a prison — it's the pressure that creates diamonds.

## The Ratio
Aim for at least **ten-to-one** (or twenty-to-one) of invented material to selected material. Generate far more than you use, then select only the most original and truest moments.
