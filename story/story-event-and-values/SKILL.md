# Story Events and Values

## What Is an Event?
"Event" means **change**. But Story Events are meaningful, not trivial.

> A STORY EVENT creates meaningful change in the life situation of a character that is expressed and experienced in terms of a **VALUE** and **ACHIEVED THROUGH CONFLICT**.

## Story Values
Universal qualities of human experience that may shift from positive to negative at any moment:
- alive/dead
- love/hate
- freedom/slavery
- truth/lie
- courage/cowardice
- loyalty/betrayal
- wisdom/stupidity
- hope/despair

Values are the **soul of storytelling**. Ultimately ours is the art of expressing a perception of values.

## Three Requirements for a Story Event
1. **Meaningful change** in a character's life situation
2. **Expressed in terms of a value** (the audience must feel the shift)
3. **Achieved through conflict** (not coincidence)

## Example: [LEGAL_DRAMA]
Rain falling on drought-stricken East Africa is powerful but not a Story Event (coincidence). A man struggling through inner, personal, social, and environmental conflict to coax rain from a cloudless sky — that storm is **majestic and sublimely meaningful** because it is change motivated through conflict.
