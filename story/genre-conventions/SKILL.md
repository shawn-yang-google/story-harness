# Genre Conventions

Genre conventions are the **specific settings, roles, events, and values** that define a particular kind of story.

## What Conventions Are
- The defining **obligations** a writer accepts when choosing a genre
- The audience enters each genre with **expectations** shaped by their experience of previous works
- Conventions include: typical settings, character roles, required events, thematic values

## How They Function
- Conventions create a **contract** between writer and audience
- The audience knows what it expects; the writer must know these expectations intimately
- Violating conventions without awareness leads to **audience dissatisfaction**
- Deliberately subverting conventions with skill leads to **surprise and delight**

## The Writer's Responsibility
Know the conventions of your genre **cold**. Only then can you work within them (delivering the expected with fresh execution) or against them (subverting expectations to create something new). Ignorance of conventions is not innovation — it's incompetence.
