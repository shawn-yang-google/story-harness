# Progressive Complication

## The Principle
To cross each gap between expectation and result, a character must take a **new, more difficult action** that carries **greater risk**. Stories progress by increasing complications and stakes.

## How It Works
1. Character faces obstacle → takes action → gap opens
2. New obstacle is **harder** than the previous one
3. New action requires **greater sacrifice or risk**
4. Stakes **escalate** — what the character stands to lose grows
5. This continues until the **ultimate crisis** demands the hardest choice

## The Prohibition
> No two scenes should repeat the same level of conflict.

If a story maintains the same level of tension without escalation, it becomes **repetitive and boring**. The audience feels the story is running in place.

## Risk
Risk is the key to progressive complication. Each action the protagonist takes must carry the possibility of **greater loss**:
- First: risk to comfort
- Then: risk to relationships
- Then: risk to livelihood
- Then: risk to identity
- Finally: risk to life (physical or spiritual)

## The Arc of Pressure
Progressive complication creates the **rising arc** of a story — from relatively simple obstacles to the seemingly impossible final challenge. This arc is what keeps the audience leaning forward.
