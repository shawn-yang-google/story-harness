# Character vs Characterization

The difference between the mask and the face beneath it.

## Characterization (The Mask)
The sum of **observable qualities**: physical appearance, mannerisms, style of speech, gestures, sexuality, age, IQ, occupation, personality, attitudes, values, where and how they live. The totality of traits makes each character unique — but it is only the surface.

## True Character (The Face)
Revealed only through **choices made under pressure**. The greater the pressure, the deeper the revelation. What a person chooses to do under **maximum pressure** is who they really are.

## The Unity
> Structure IS character; character IS structure. They're the same thing.

The event structure of a story is created from choices made under pressure. Characters cannot be developed apart from structure; structure cannot be divorced from character. They are **mirror reflections** of each other.

## The Key Test
A character may appear kind, generous, and honest (characterization) — but under pressure, do they remain so? The gap between characterization and true character is where **drama lives**.
