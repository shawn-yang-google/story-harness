# The Law of Conflict

## The Law
> Nothing moves forward in a story except through **conflict**.

## Source of Conflict
Conflict arises from **The Gap** — when the world resists the character's will. The character takes action expecting one result and gets another, forcing a new, more difficult action.

## Three Levels of Conflict
1. **Inner Conflict**: The character vs aspects of their own nature — mind, body, emotions
2. **[CLASSIC_FILM]l Conflict**: The character vs other people — family, friends, lovers
3. **Extra-[CLASSIC_FILM]l Conflict**: The character vs society, institutions, or the physical environment

## Why It's a Law, Not a Guideline
- Without conflict, values do not change
- Without value change, scenes are non-events
- Without events, there is no story
- **Conflict is the oxygen of story** — remove it and the story suffocates

## Common Violations
- Scenes of "pleasant agreement" where characters chat without friction
- Characters who get what they want without struggle
- Problems that resolve themselves through coincidence
- Exposition dumped through conflict-free dialogue

## The Escalation Principle
Each new conflict must be **more intense** than the last. The character's successive actions carry greater **risk** as the story progresses.
