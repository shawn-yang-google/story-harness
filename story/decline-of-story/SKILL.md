# The Decline of Story

Story is humanity's most prolific art form, yet overall quality is eroding.

## The Erosion
- When storytelling goes bad, the result is **decadence** — spectacle for substance, trickery for truth
- Weak stories degenerate into multimillion-dollar razzle-dazzle demo reels
- A culture cannot evolve without honest, powerful storytelling

## Why [FILM_INDUSTRY_CENTER] Thrives
Not because of quality but because it has **virtually no competition**. European filmmakers lost the craft of their predecessors ([MASTER_DIRECTOR], [MASTER_DIRECTOR], [MASTER_DIRECTOR]). Asian filmmakers now seize the spotlight because they **tell superb stories**.

## The Root Cause
The erosion of values has brought a corresponding erosion of story. Unlike writers in the past, we can assume nothing — we must dig deeply into life to uncover new insights, then create story vehicles for an increasingly skeptical world.
