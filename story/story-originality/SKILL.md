# Story Originality

Originality is the **confluence of content and form** — distinctive choices of subject plus a unique shaping of the telling.

## Content and Form Are Interdependent
- **Content** (setting, characters, ideas) and **form** (selection and arrangement of events) require, inspire, and mutually influence one another
- If content is cliché, the telling will be cliché
- If the story design is innovative, settings, characters, and ideas must be equally fresh

## Eccentricity ≠ Originality
Difference for the sake of difference is as empty as slavishly following commercial imperatives. The "well-made" formula may choke a story's voice, but "art movie" quirkiness will give it a speech impediment.

> A mature artist never calls attention to himself, and a wise artist never does anything merely because it breaks convention.

## The Master Metaphor
The storyteller's selection and arrangement of events is his **master metaphor** for the interconnectedness of all levels of reality — personal, political, environmental, spiritual. Story structure reveals the writer's personal cosmology, his map of life's hidden order.

## The Goal
Each great writer steps out of the crowd because each selects a content like no one else, designs a form like no one else, combining the two into a style unmistakably their own.
