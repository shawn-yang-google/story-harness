# The Story Triangle

The far corners of story art create a **triangle of formal possibilities** that maps the universe of stories.

```
              ARCHPLOT
            (Classical Design)
           Causality, Closed Ending
          Linear Time, External Conflict
         Single Protagonist, Active Protagonist
            Consistent Reality

    MINIPLOT                    ANTIPLOT
  (Minimalism)              (Anti-Structure)
  Open Ending                Coincidence
  Internal Conflict          Nonlinear Time
  Multi-Protagonists         Inconsistent Realities
  Passive Protagonist
```

## Archplot (Top)
The meat, potatoes, pasta, rice, and couscous of world cinema. Has informed the vast majority of internationally successful films for 100 years. Principles are "classical" — timeless and transcultural, reaching back through millennia.

## Miniplot (Bottom-Left)
Begins with Classical Design elements but **reduces** them — shrinking external conflict, shifting to internal struggle, opening endings. Strives for simplicity and economy while retaining enough of the classical to satisfy audiences.

## Antiplot (Bottom-Right)
The cinematic counterpart to the anti-novel and Theatre of the Absurd. **Reverses** classical forms — contradicting traditional principles to exploit or ridicule the very idea of formal structure. Tends toward extravagance and self-conscious overstatement.

## Key Insight
To understand your place in this universe, study the coordinates, compare them to your work-in-progress, and let them guide you to that point you share with other writers of a similar vision.
