# The Story Spine

## Definition
The **spine** of a story is the deep desire and effort of the protagonist that drives through all other elements. It is the unifying through-line from inciting incident to story climax.

## How It's Created
The **inciting incident** arouses a desire in the protagonist — this desire becomes the spine. Every scene, sequence, and act relates to this central drive, either advancing or obstructing it.

## What It Does
- **Holds the story together** — gives unity to diverse events
- **Provides direction** — the audience always knows what the story is about
- **Creates focus** — scenes that don't connect to the spine should be questioned
- **Sustains momentum** — the unresolved desire pulls the audience forward

## The Test
For any scene, ask: **Does this connect to the protagonist's central desire?** If the scene doesn't advance, obstruct, or complicate the spine, it may not belong in the story.

## Spine vs Subplots
Subplots have their own mini-spines (driven by their own inciting incidents), but these must ultimately **serve the main spine** — enriching, complicating, or contrasting with the protagonist's central quest.
