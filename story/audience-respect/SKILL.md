# Audience Respect

Story is about **respect, not disdain**, for the audience.

## Why Talented People Write Badly
- They're **blinded by an idea** they feel compelled to prove, OR
- They're **driven by an emotion** they must express

## Why Talented People Write Well
They're moved by a **desire to touch the audience**.

## The Audience's Power
- Audiences are **amazingly sensitive** — masks fall away, faces become vulnerable and receptive
- The collective IQ of an audience **jumps twenty-five points** when they settle into a darkened theatre
- The audience is **smarter than most films** — they see endings coming, predict character behavior

## The Design Imperative
No film can work without understanding audience reactions and anticipations. You must shape story to **both express your vision AND satisfy the audience's desires**. The audience is a force as determining of story design as any other element — without it, the creative act is pointless.

> It's all a writer can do, using every bit of craft he's mastered, to keep ahead of the sharp perceptions of a focused audience.
