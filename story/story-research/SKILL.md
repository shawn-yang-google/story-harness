# Story Research

To win the **war on cliché**, a writer must possess commanding knowledge of their story's world. This is achieved through three types of research:

## 1. Memory
Exploring **personal experiences** that touch on the characters' lives. What do you know from your own life that connects to this world? Mine your autobiography for emotional truths and sensory details.

## 2. Imagination
**Living the character's life** in vivid detail, hour by hour. Ask: What would it be like to wake as this person? What do they see, smell, fear, desire? Seek hidden connections between the imagined life and universal human experience.

## 3. Fact
Gathering knowledge from **external sources** — libraries, interviews, locations, documents. Factual research stimulates talent and expands the circle of experience. It provides the authentic details that make a fictional world convincing.

## The War on Cliché
Research is ammunition. The more deeply you know your story's world, the more **original and specific** your creative choices become. Ignorance breeds cliché; knowledge breeds originality.
