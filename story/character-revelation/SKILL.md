# Character Revelation

## The Principle
True character is revealed through the **choices a person makes under pressure**. The greater the pressure, the deeper and truer the revelation of the character's essential nature.

## How It Works
- Under **mild pressure**, we see surface choices — polite, predictable
- Under **moderate pressure**, cracks appear in the mask — the character surprises us
- Under **maximum pressure**, the deepest truth emerges — who this person really is

## What Doesn't Reveal Character
- **Dialogue**: Characters can say anything — words can deceive
- **Backstory**: Past events explain but don't prove present nature
- **Appearance**: The mask of characterization
- **Habits and mannerisms**: Behavioral decoration

## What Does Reveal Character
**Dilemmas** — moments where the character must choose between two paths, both with consequences. The choice reveals values, priorities, courage or cowardice, selfishness or sacrifice.

## Design Principle
Create scenes of increasing pressure that force characters into progressively harder choices. Each choice strips away another layer of the mask until the audience sees the character's core.
