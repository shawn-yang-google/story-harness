# Third-Party Content Notice

## Robert McKee's Narrative Theory

The skill files in the following directories contain AI-generated summaries and
reinterpretations of narrative craft principles originally taught by Robert McKee
and published in his books:

- **`character/`** — Based on concepts from *Character: The Art of Role and Cast Design for Page, Stage, and Screen* (Robert McKee, 2021)
- **`dialogue/`** — Based on concepts from *Dialogue: The Art of Verbal Action for Page, Stage, and Screen* (Robert McKee, 2016)
- **`story/`** — Based on concepts from *Story: Substance, Structure, Style and the Principles of Screenwriting* (Robert McKee, 1997)

### What This Means

These skill files are **derivative works** — they do not contain direct
quotations from McKee's texts, but they summarize, reorganize, and reinterpret
his narrative theory principles for use as AI prompts and evaluation criteria.

The underlying **ideas, principles, and techniques** (e.g., "subtext in
dialogue," "mask vs. true character," "turning values in scenes") are part of
the general body of narrative craft knowledge and are not copyrightable. However,
McKee's specific **expression, organization, and articulation** of these ideas
in his books is protected by copyright.

### Fair Use Considerations

We believe the use of these summaries falls within fair use because:

1. **Transformative purpose** — The skill files transform McKee's prose
   explanations into structured AI evaluation criteria and prompt templates,
   serving a fundamentally different purpose than the original works.
2. **No market substitution** — These files cannot substitute for reading
   McKee's books. They lack the examples, case studies, exercises, and narrative
   depth that make the books valuable.
3. **Limited portion** — Each skill file distills a single concept into a
   concise rubric, not a reproduction of a chapter or section.

### Attribution

All narrative craft principles in these directories are attributed to Robert

## AutoHarness Paper

The training methodology (tree-search with Thompson sampling for harness
synthesis) is adapted from:

- Hu, E. Z., et al. (2025). *AutoHarness: Improving LLM Agents by Automatically Synthesizing a Code Harness*. [arXiv:2603.03329](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03329)

The paper's methodology was adapted for narrative evaluation (from game
environments to story verification). The implementation is original.
