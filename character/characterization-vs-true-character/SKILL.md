# Characterization vs. True Character

Distinguish between observable traits and the inner nature revealed through pressure.

- Define characterization as the composite of observable traits: age, gender, race, speech, gestures, job, dress, and personality.
- Treat characterization as the "masks" or personae a character wears in their relationships.
- Define true character as the unseen inner nature—the deepest motivations and rock-bottom values.
- Reveal true character only when the character is confronted by significant pressure.
- Focus on choices made and actions taken under pressure to express core identity.
- Use characterization to provide clues to identity while recognizing that appearance is not reality.
- Combine both characterization and true character to create a believable role.
- Ensure the character's essential self makes choices that are consistent with their pursue of desire.
