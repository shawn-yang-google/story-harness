# Genre and Medium as Inspiration

Choice of medium and genre not only influences your story's shape but determines the traits and behaviors your characters can express.

### Medium Shapes Character

- **Screen**: Characters act more visually than verbally. Create characters who express themselves with looks and gestures.
- **Stage**: Characters act more verbally than visually. Endow characters with expressive, layered dialogue.
- **Page**: Prose narrators usually speak in past tense, recalling events retrospectively. Give first-person narrators keen memories, discerning eyes, and sensitivity to subtext.

### Genre Shapes Character

- True first sparks more often flash from fiction than from reality — a phrase of dialogue or a beautifully turned image in a novel, play, film, or series.
- Pay attention to your story-going habits: What do you binge? Which novels grip you? Let your genre passions inspire early character intuitions.
- Each genre has conventions that define the range of character possibilities — respect them, then push beyond.

### Application

- Move freely between media. Shrinking your writerly identity to a single medium limits creative range.
- If no one produces your screenplay, rework it as a play or novel — air your characters in public and watch skills grow.
- Make certain the medium that inspires you harmonizes with more than a lifestyle — it must connect to the life demanding expression inside you.
