---

### Distinguish Genre Classes
Divide genres into Primary (creating content: characters, events, values) and Presentational (expressing content: style, tone, media).

### Select Plots of Fortune (Outer Change)
Identify the mother form to determine outer life conditions:
1. **Action**: Life vs. Death. Core event: Hero at mercy of villain.
2. **Horror**: Survival vs. Damnation. Core event: Hero at mercy of monster.
3. **Crime**: Justice vs. Injustice. Core event: Hero at mercy of villain.
4. **Love**: Commitment vs. Loss. Core event: Act of love.
5. **Domestic**: Unity vs. Breakup. Core event: Family unifies or splinters.
6. **War**: Victory vs. Defeat. Core event: Decisive battle.
7. **Social**: Problem vs. Solution. Core event: Recognition of crisis.
8. **Political**: Power vs. Powerless. Core event: Power won or lost.
9. **Modern Epic**: Tyranny vs. Freedom. Core event: Act of rebellion.
10. **Enterprise**: Success vs. Failure. Core event: Professional loss.

### Select Plots of Character (Inner Change)
Arc the protagonist's inner nature across one of three qualities:
1. **Redemption (Morality)**: Inner nature moves from negative to positive.
2. **Degeneration (Morality)**: Inner nature moves from positive to negative.
3. **Education (Mentality)**: Moves from meaninglessness to finding purpose.
4. **Disillusionment (Mentality)**: Moves from optimism to fatalism/loss of belief.
5. **Evolution (Humanity)**: Protagonist becomes a fuller human being.
6. **Devolution (Humanity)**: Protagonist becomes a lesser human being.

### Apply Presentational Genres
Shape the performance using one of ten techniques: Comedy, Musical, Science Fiction, History, Fantasy, Documentary, Animation, Autobiography, Biography, or High Art.

### Combine Genres
Mix or merge genres to expand character complexity. Use subplots to add dimensions by crossing conventions from different genres.
