# Character and Time

Storytelling spatializes time. From a character's point of view, time pours from a half-remembered past into an ocean of unknown futures. From the reader's point of view, time freezes within the parentheses of first and last images.

## Story as Being, Character as Becoming
- A story is a metaphor for life that expresses the nature of **being**: once told, it stands like a work of temporal sculpture in a state of permanence.
- A character is a metaphor for humanity that expresses the nature of **becoming**: she changes and reshapes through conflict until the climax sends her into a future altered in substance and circumstance.
- Story unfolds event by event; character arcs through transformation.

## The Observing Mind
- Because the author has frozen time's flow, the reader's mind skates freely back and forth through days, months, years.
- The reader traces story lines to their roots, unearths causes buried in the past, and prophesizes outcomes before the character's fate arrives.

## Ideas Rust, Character Lasts
- Ideas have a life span, often short. Stories tend to rust, and the more era-bound their meanings, the shorter their existence.
- Even great stories survive only when their themes receive constant, up-to-the-now reinterpretation.
- **What lasts is character.**

## Application
- Build characters whose arc of becoming transcends the story's temporal setting.
- Invest in dimensionality and transformation over topical theme, because character endures where idea decays.
