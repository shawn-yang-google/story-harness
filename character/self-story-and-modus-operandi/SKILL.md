# Self-Story and Modus Operandi

Define a character's habitual action patterns by constructing their internal operating system.

### The Self-Story
- **Identity**: Synthesize past, present, and future tales into a unified identity.
- **The Future Story**: Focus on the character's "ideal future" as the primary driver of current actions.
- **Rationalization**: Understand how they justify their past to explain who they have become.

### Modus Operandi (MO)
- **Action Patterns**: Identify the unique ways a character habitually pursues desires or escapes fears.
- **Tactics**: Distinguish the MO from personality traits like dress or mood.

### Common MO Themes
- **[FILM_INDUSTRY_CENTER]**: Mystery Lover (seeks enigma), Alien Adventurer (feels estranged), Fairy Tale (idealized prince/princess).
- **Political**: Tyranny (oppresses others), Democracy (balances power), Anarchy (creates chaos).
- **Object**: Collector (treasures things/people), Player (treats life as a game), Obsessive (single-minded addiction).

### Putting Character Into Action
- **Scene-Objective**: Define what the character wants *now* to move toward their Super-Objective.
- **First Action**: Launch with a tried-and-true tactic based on the character's sense of probability.
- **Surprise**: Use counteractions to violate expectations and force deeper, more desperate actions.
- **Evolution**: Escalate pressure until the character's core identity or morality bends.
