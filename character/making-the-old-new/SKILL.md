# Making the Old New

Reinvent familiar archetypes and stock characters by creating unique, fresh identities within known traditions.

### Four Paths to Originality
1. **Speculative Fiction**: Create plausible motivations for implausible actions. Ground the supernatural in human needs.
2. **Realism**: Design off-center characters who view the world through a non-traditional lens.
3. **Radical Shocks**: Use extreme experiences to force characters to abandon old beliefs for new ones.
4. **Immediate POV**: Write in-character to add urgency. Use past POV for prose and present "now" for screen/stage.

### Refreshing Stocks and Types
- **Characterization**: Give stock roles (lawyers, doctors) one-of-a-kind techniques or idiosyncrasies to carry out their tasks.
- **Subvert Expectations**: Merge polar opposites, such as the autocracy of myth with the psychological freedom of modern fiction.
- **Case Study ([FANTASY_EPIC_SERIES])**: Merge high fantasy with harsh realism. Use shades of gray to bridge the black-and-white outlines of symbolic figures.
- **Evolution**: Transform static archetypes by placing them under modern pressures (e.g., turning a machine-like assassin into a unique human/machine hybrid).

### Execution
- **Avoid Cliché**: Treat "used" ideas with fresh originality.
- **Purpose**: Ensure instant recognition of a character's role while surprising the audience with their specific implementation.
