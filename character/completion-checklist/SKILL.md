# Completion Checklist

A completed character has nothing left to reveal, nothing left to change. Use this checklist to verify that a character has been taken to the absolute limits of her humanity.

## Need vs. Desire
- **Desire**: the character's conscious, persistent purpose—her unreached goal pursued throughout the telling.
- **Need**: an empty inner space, a potential that craves realization. Only the author perceives it. The character is never aware of her own need.
- Verify that these are distinct. Desire drives the plot; need drives completion.

## Preparation Check
- Is the character incomplete at the inciting incident? Has she never experienced her capacities to their absolute limit?
- Can you articulate the void: unthought intelligence, underfelt emotion, unused talent, underlived life?
- Is the inciting incident perfectly tuned to the protagonist's specific need?

## Revelation Check
- Does the story's dynamic of events reveal true character in contrast with or contradiction to characterization?
- Can you answer: Who do we *think* she is at the beginning? Who do we *know* she is by the climax?
- Has maximum risk been applied to strip away personae and expose the core self?

## Change Check (if applicable)
- Does change arc along the defining dimension?
- Does the arc move in dynamic zigzags, not monotone progression?
- Is the change credible—rooted in the character's established nature and capacities?
- Characters who do not change (action heroes, comic obsessives) have no need. Verify this is intentional.

## Completion Check
- At the climax, does the character experience herself at the limit of her emotional and mental capacities?
- Has the author explored and expressed the core self at its greatest depths?
- Is there nothing left to reveal, nothing left to change?
- Study the character in the days before the story begins and ask: given the small percentage of aptitude she's used thus far, what does her humanity lack? Does the story answer that lack?

## Final Questions
- What event would carry her to the limits of her greatest thoughts, her deepest being?
- What pressures, conflicts, choices, and reactions take her humanity to its fullest?
- Once completed, does the character's reflection carry the weight of ironic fulfillment?
