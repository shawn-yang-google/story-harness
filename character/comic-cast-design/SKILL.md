# Comic Cast Design

Comic characters are marked by a blind obsession — a rigid behavior they cannot see in themselves and from which they cannot deviate. Comedy arises when these obsessions collide.

### Case Study: [COMEDY_FILM]

**[ANXIOUS_LAWYER]** (3 dimensions) — Obsesses on fear of embarrassment.
- Fearful / Courageous: At first frightened of [PSEUDO_INTELLECTUAL], eventually finds courage to face him.
- Lawyer / Criminal: Devoted to the law but commits crimes for love.
- Devoted to others / Then to himself: Works for wife, daughter, and clients, then finally fights for what he wants.

**[MANIPULATIVE_WOMAN]** (2 dimensions) — Obsesses on men who speak foreign languages.
- Sexually calculating / Sexually impulsive: Uses charms to manipulate, occasionally falling prey to her own blind spot.
- Devoted to self / Then to [ANXIOUS_LAWYER]: Money is her only object of desire, then she gives her heart.

**[PSEUDO_INTELLECTUAL]** (2 dimensions) — Obsesses on [EXISTENTIAL_PHILOSOPHER].
- Intelligent / Ignorant: Quotes philosophy but thinks the [TRANSIT_SYSTEM] is a political movement.
- Clever / Confused: Thinks quickly under pressure but loses focus in conversation.

**[ANIMAL_LOVER]** (1 dimension) — Obsesses on animal welfare.
- Loves animals to the point of obsession but disdains people — feels tragic loss when an animal dies but happily kills a human.

### Design Principles

- Give each comic character one blind obsession they cannot see in themselves.
- Build dimensions from the gap between what the character believes about themselves and what the audience sees.
- Let obsessions collide between characters to generate comic conflict.
- Even comedy makes meaning: the controlling idea of *[MANIPULATIVE_WOMAN]* is that you could end up with riches and love if willing to throw away career and family.

### Application

- Map each comic character's obsession, then design dimensions that contradict it. The more rigid the obsession, the funnier the collision with reality.
