# Plot-Driven vs. Character-Driven

Determine the dominant causality of your story by understanding the six key differences.

1. **Causality**:
    - In plot-driven stories, trigger events from outside the characters (nature, society, chance).
    - In character-driven stories, derive events from the characters' conscious and subconscious choices.
2. **Identity**:
    - Assign desires that originate externally for plot-driven protagonists.
    - Foster desires that originate internally for character-driven protagonists.
3. **Values**:
    - Focus on global values (Peace/War, Justice/Injustice) in plot-driven tales.
    - Focus on personal values (Love/Hate, Truth/Lie) in character-driven tales.
4. **Depth**:
    - Enrich plot-driven stories with social and physical settings.
    - Layer character-driven stories with psychological contradictions and subconscious impulses.
5. **Curiosity**:
    - In plot-driven works, ask "How will they do it?" using radical weaponry or tactics.
    - In character-driven works, ask "What will they do?" through unpredictable human behavior.
6. **Freedom vs. Fate**:
    - View plot-driven characters as being on an inevitable, fated path determined by their rigid natures.
    - View character-driven stories as being shaped by the freedom of choice, where multiple futures are possible.

Balance both causalities to reflect the complexity of life.
