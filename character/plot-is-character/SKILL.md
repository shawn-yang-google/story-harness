# Plot is Character

Accept that plot and character are essentially the same. Recognize that an event without a character is a nonevent, and a character without events is merely a static portrait.

- Treat plot as the arrangement of events and character as the being who causes or reacts to them.
- Understand that a role only becomes a character when an event brings their actions to life.
- Redesign events to change a character's nature or express who they have become.
- Reinvent character psychology to change events, ensuring characters make convincing new choices.
- Apply Henry James's principle: character is the determination of incident, and incident is the illustration of character.
- Analyze turning points as both revelations of character and plot reversals, as seen in the "incest" revelation in *[NOIR_MYSTERY]*.
- Use a character's fatal flaw to drive their decisions and turn the plot in a specific direction.
