# Five Spheres of Inspiration

Picture the storyteller's universe as five concentric spheres nested one inside the other. Use them to trace inspiration from the outside world inward to the creative self.

### The Five Spheres (Outermost to Innermost)

1. **Actuality**: Time, place, people, things — past and present reality. Random sensations or observations collide with imagination to trigger character creation.
2. **Medium**: Page, stage, or screen. Choice of medium shapes character traits and behaviors — screen characters act visually, stage characters act verbally, prose narrators recall retrospectively.
3. **Genre**: The storytelling genres and their conventions. Pay attention to your own story-going habits — the genre you love should inspire your early character intuitions.
4. **Story Events**: Backstory, turning points, and themes. Use the "Magic If" — ask "What would happen if...?" to spark inciting incidents that in turn inspire the characters who react to them.
5. **The Author's Creative Self**: At the epicenter stands the writer, looking outward for inspiration and inward for truth.

### Application

- Begin on the outside and work inward. Let each sphere filter and refine your character instinct.
- Recognize that inspiration rarely blazes into a finished cast — it sparks a sliver of intuition that chain-reacts through free association.
- When early breakthroughs dead-end in cliché, persist — precious insights trace a winding trail to success.
- Move freely between spheres; shrinking your identity to a single source limits creative range.
