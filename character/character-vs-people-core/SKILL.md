# Character vs. People Core

The fundamental distinction between reality and fiction lies in the nature of the "person." Humans are evolving, opaque works-in-progress; characters are finished, translucent works-in-performance.

## Distilled Principles

*   **Work-in-Progress vs. Work-in-Performance**: Real people change until death; characters are fixed within the parentheses of their story. They stay who they become at the climax forever.
*   **Experience vs. Expression**: Humans experience far more than they express. Characters must express everything they experience to be known to the core.
*   **Representation vs. Symbolism**: People represent themselves and their social lives; characters symbolize the human spirit and live within a choreographed cast.
*   **Opacity vs. Accessibility**: Real people are often opaque and difficult to truly know. Brilliantly dramatized characters are clearer, more complex, and more accessible than anyone in reality.
*   **The Container of Story**: A character enters a story as a "canister of the past" and a "sponge for the future," designed to reveal their nature in full.

## Practical Application

*   **Focus on Expression**: Ensure characters do not harbor internal experiences that never manifest in action or dialogue.
*   **Define the Arc**: Treat the character's journey as a movement from "becoming" to a fixed state of "being" by the story's end.
*   **Elevate the Ordinary**: Transform the "human stuff" of inspiration into a layered, dimensional figure that competes with humanity through heightened presence.
*   **Use Symbolism**: Ask what aspect of the human spirit the character embodies, rather than just copying a real person's traits.
