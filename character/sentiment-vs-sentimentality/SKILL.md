# Sentiment vs. Sentimentality

Master the distinction between earned, balanced emotion and cheap, forced manipulation.

### Sentiment (Earned Emotion)
- **Proportion**: Ensure the emotional response is in balance with the events that caused it.
- **Motivation**: Ground feelings in creditable causes and powerful actions.
- **Example**: A character sacrificing their life for love, in direct proportion to that love.

### Sentimentality (Cheap Emotion)
- **Imbalance**: Avoid overexpressed indulgence out of proportion to the trigger.
- **Manipulation**: Reject phony causes used to trigger false effects (e.g., forced happy endings).
- **Phony Symbols**: Do not use "kitten-stroking" villains or dead children in red coats solely for wish fulfillment.
- **Danger**: Recognize that sentimentality often masks or sweetens underlying cruelty.

### Testing for Sentiment
- **Measure Motivation**: Ask if the action taken justifies the intensity of the feeling.
- **Verify Value Change**: Ensure emotion is the side effect of a genuine change in value (negative to positive or vice versa).
- **Resist Easy Wins**: Challenge "sweetening" characters to make them likable without earning it through action.
- **Audit Proportions**: Scale the audience's emotional payoff to the character's actual sacrifice or struggle.
