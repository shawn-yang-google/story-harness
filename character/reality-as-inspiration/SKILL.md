# Reality as Inspiration

People are to writers what sounds are to musicians. Use human nature as raw material, but never copy — reimagine real people into intriguing characters.

### Key Principles

- **Real vs. Fictional**: A real person is enigmatic and incomplete — a life yet to be lived. A character is an artwork — a complete, expressive version of its original inspiration.
- **Depth over Snapshot**: An acquaintance turned into a character can be as superficial as a snapshot. Look beneath the surface, uncover silent truths, surprise with insights.
- **Credibility through Belief**: If you don't trust or believe in someone, you won't feel compelled to write them. Let credibility grow trait after trait until belief deepens.
- **The Telling Detail**: One unique facet can suddenly imply an entire humanity — this "telling detail" lifts an interesting inspiration out of the *what is* into the *what could be*.

### Application

- Never draw directly from life without transformation. Reimagine real people into characters readers can believe in.
- Build global knowledge of your character's setting, society, and traits — generalization shrivels belief and invention.
- Seek the collision between the density of reality (objects, images, sounds, odors, textures) and a boundless imagination.
- Characters just glide on by while people confront us. Use this distance to achieve godlike knowing that objective observation cannot reach.
