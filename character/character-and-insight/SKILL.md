# Character and Insight

Characters fill us with insight because they stand still while we study them. Unlike real people, whose subconscious stays stubbornly sub, characters allow us to perceive depths impossible in daily life.

## The Insight Advantage
- A psychic power seems to carry us through a character's words and deeds, down to unspoken thoughts and desires, into the silent currents of the subconscious mind.
- When we turn our gaze on ourselves, our subconscious remains hidden. Characters offer what self-examination cannot.
- As [CLASSICAL_POET] wrote: "Oh, would some power the gift to give us, to see ourselves as others see us."
- A cast of characters offers a kind of group therapy.

## Aesthetic Angles of Perception
- Characters lean into their futures, awareness narrowed by pursuit.
- Readers and audiences first lean back to survey the 360-degree world encircling the cast, then lean forward to peer into psychological depths.
- These aesthetic angles grant insight into characters and their societies often better than we see into ourselves and our own.

## The [CHEMIST_CRIMINAL] Principle
- A well-constructed character can be understood more deeply than any real person.
- "I often wish I understood myself and the United States as well as I understand [CHEMIST_CRIMINAL] and *[CRIME_DRAMA_SERIES]*."
- The character becomes a mirror that reflects truths about the self and society simultaneously.

## Application
- Design characters whose inner depths reward sustained examination.
- Layer subconscious currents beneath surface actions to create insight-generating tension.
- Use the gap between a character's self-awareness and the reader's perception as the primary engine of insight.
