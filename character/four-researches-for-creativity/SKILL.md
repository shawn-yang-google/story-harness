# Four Researches for Creativity

Feed your creativity and avoid clichéd characters by traveling down the four roads of research.

1. **[CLASSIC_FILM]l Research (Memory)**:
    - Inventory your memory for meaningful emotional experiences.
    - Re-create past scenes in a vivid, diary-like way, focusing on inner sensations and physical behavior.
    - Transfer or transform your own emotional truths into your characters' lives.
2. **Imaginative Research (Analogy)**:
    - Use the power of analogy to invent new wholes out of bits of the past.
    - Take characters for "long walks" through your imagination to gain overall impressions before analyzing motives.
    - Fuse random thoughts, dreams, and observations into unique character fusions.
3. **Book-Bound Research (Factual)**:
    - Study psychology and sociology related to your characters' relationships and situations.
    - Use academic learning to find insights you couldn't discover on your own.
    - Ensure your specific characters become universal by grounding them in common human stage and pressures.
4. **Grounded Research (Actual)**:
    - Locate your story's settings in the real world.
    - Observe behaviors, talk to people who live those events, and take documentary-style notes.
    - Become an absolute authority on your characters and their settings through field observation.
