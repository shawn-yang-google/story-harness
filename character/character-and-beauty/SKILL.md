# Character and Beauty

When a character's traits and depths align seamlessly, she emanates beauty. Beauty is not prettiness. Pretty is decorative; beautiful is expressive.

## Philosophical Definitions of Beauty
- **Harmony** ([ANCIENT_PHILOSOPHER]): seamless integration of parts into a meaningful whole.
- **Radiance** ([MEDIEVAL_PHILOSOPHER]): an inner light that shines through form.
- **Sublimity** (Elijah Jordan): awe that transcends the surface.
- **Clarity and repose** (John Ruskin): settled stillness of expression.
- **A deedless calm** ([MODERN_PHILOSOPHER]): beauty that emanates from fine art no matter how turbulent or dark the work.

## Beauty Beyond Appearance
- A character may be villainous, even Horror-film ugly, but when her traits harmonize into a meaningful whole, she radiates a kind of beauty, however grotesque.
- Beauty in character is structural, not cosmetic. It arises from the coherence of contradictions, not from pleasant surfaces.

## Beauty and Love
- As [ANCIENT_PHILOSOPHER] taught, our response to beauty feels much like love.
- The pleasure we take in a superbly crafted character is more than a matter of judgment—it's a sense of affection.
- Beauty amplifies our inner life; kitsch deafens it.

## Application
- Align a character's outer traits with inner depths to produce expressive coherence.
- Do not confuse attractiveness with beauty. Even monstrous characters can be beautiful when their dimensions harmonize.
- Seek the radiance that comes from internal consistency across all planes—characterization, true character, and subconscious.
