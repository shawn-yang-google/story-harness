# Observer and Observed

Master the duality of self-awareness to alternate between analytical distance and empathetic immersion.

### Key Principles

- **Differentiate Selves**: Distinguish between the "Core Self" (the observer who watches and understands) and the "Agent Self" (the observed who acts in the world).
- **Pivot Awareness**: Move from awareness to action, and then back to awareness, creating an inner theater of self-criticism and self-congratulation.
- **Step Back**: Use the mind's ability to see itself as an object to analyze character behavior from the outside.
- **Maintain Health**: Recognize that peak self-awareness and creative focus radiate from a well-nourished mind and body.
- **Use Mirror Images**: Glimpse the core self observing the agent self before a reaction occurs to understand the split-second nature of consciousness.
- **Synthesize Perspectives**: Combine the protective spirit of the observer with the active performances of the agent to build depth.
