# Motivation vs. Desire

Motivations and desires form two very different energies. Motivations plant footings in the past and push from behind; desires anchor the future and pull forward.

### Twelve Inside-Out Motivations (Primal to Elevated)

1. **Immortality**: Dread of death inspires the wish to leave something of value.
2. **Survival**: The imperative to act toward the positive as one perceives it.
3. **Balance**: The brain's tiny scale weighs positive vs. negative, craving equilibrium.
4. **Pleasure**: The irresistible craving, even when future pain is certain.
5. **Sex**: [PSYCHOLOGIST]'s contention that sexual instincts drive all objectives.
6. **Power**: [PSYCHOLOGIST]'s theory that desire for power focuses all strivings.
7. **Empathy**: The hunger to belong that creates tribes and shared suffering.
8. **More**: Greed (craving more), Envy (tortured by others' possessions), Jealousy (a rival enters).
9. **Interest**: The mind prefers doing things for their own sake — means as its own end.
10. **Meaning**: A meaningless life is a life of vertigo, dizzily out of control.
11. **Fulfillment**: The longing to plumb untapped depths and fulfill inner promise.
12. **Transcendence**: [PSYCHOLOGIST]'s subconscious longing for godlike knowledge beyond reach.

### Outside-In Motivations

- Social institutions influence trends but cannot be imposed directly on characters. Poverty and wealth are conditions, not causes.
- External forces must work through each unique personality — who knows what will come out the other side.

### The Object of Desire

- An inciting incident throws life out of balance. The super-objective (restoring equilibrium) focuses on a specific object of desire.
- A motivation is an unfocused subconscious latency until an inciting incident spurs it to life and focuses it on a desire.
