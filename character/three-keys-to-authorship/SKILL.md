# Three Keys to Authorship

Focusing on these three faculties allows a writer to develop a unique, reflective understanding of character behavior.

## 1. Moral Imagination
- Sensitivity to life's values and the recognition of shifting charges (positive vs. negative) and intensities.
- Identifying and measuring values at play (Honesty vs. Dishonesty, Kindness vs. Cruelty) within characters and settings.

## 2. Logical Implication
- The ability to take a hint and imagine a human; given a fingertip, take an arm; given an arm, take an army.
- Developing deductive and inductive powers to work from a piece into a whole (child to tribe) or a whole to a piece (city to lost soul).

## 3. Self-Knowledge
- All writing is autobiographical. The author is the only human being they will ever know in depth and breadth.
- Penetrating the mysteries of your own humanity allows you to understand the humanity of others.
- Asking "If I were this character in these circumstances, what would I do?" leads to truthful, human answers.
- We are all far more alike than different; our fundamental human experiences are shared.
