# Nine Subtraction Techniques

To radicalize a character, empty out her conventional aspects. Each deletion pushes her toward the extreme, creating characters that inhabit the boundary between meaning and absurdity.

## 1. Subtract Self-Awareness
- The self-aware dramatic character can think "this could get me into real trouble" and go on anyway. Remove that awareness and she becomes a comic obsessive.
- The comic mind is ruled by a blind obsession: single-minded as a stone, persistent as the sea, repetitious as rain.
- Conventional obsessives retain some commonsense interests: [ANXIOUS_LAWYER] fears embarrassment (*[COMEDY_FILM]*); [COMEDY_CREATOR] obsesses on social propriety (*[COMEDY_SERIES]*).
- To radicalize: take away everything except the obsession and never let her out. [RADICAL_PLAYWRIGHT] traps the maids in sadomasochistic games; [MODERN_PLAYWRIGHT] traps [MINOR_CHARACTER_A] and [MINOR_CHARACTER_B] in [TRAGIC_PRINCE]'s story.

## 2. Subtract Depth
- Empty out a character's humanity until she shrinks into an inner void.
- Peel away sanity ([FRUSTRATED_MAN] in *[FILM_DRAMA]*), morality ([LITERARY_NONFICTION_WRITER] in *[LITERARY_NONFICTION_WRITER]*), or core self ([GENIUS_MATHEMATICIAN] in *[BIOGRAPHICAL_FILM]*).
- Despair swallows humanity and leads life to the brink of absurdity.

## 3. Subtract Change
- Flat characters contract into themselves, eliminating ties to other characters.
- In Realism, flat characters are revealed but incapable of change ([TRAGIC_PROTAGONIST], [TRAGIC_PROTAGONIST] in [VICTORIAN_NOVELIST]).
- In non-Realism, heroes personify Good or Evil and stay so until they die.
- If a radical character living in a radical world were to change from flat to complex, the switch would seem false. [WAITING_CHARACTER_A] and [WAITING_CHARACTER_B]'s "Nothing to be done" cannot give way to ambition without destroying the work.

## 4. Subtract Identity
- Remove the cultural threads that compose identity: gender, class, family, religion, nationality.
- **Loss of an identity source**: [MAD_WOMAN] in *[POSTCOLONIAL_NOVEL]*, stripped of her Creole identity, goes mad.
- **Rejection of an identity source**: [ANGRY_YOUNG_MAN] [CHARACTER_NAME_PORTER] in *[MODERN_PLAY]* realizes his chance for an epic life is lost with the British Empire.
- **Trauma**: [AMNESIAC_ASSASSIN]'s amnesia, [FALL_FROM_GRACE_DRAMA]'s delusional breakdown.
- **Identity theft**: [IDENTITY_THIEF] in *[THRILLER_FILM]* steals her roommate's identity to fill her own void.
- **Obsession**: The rebel in *[ESPIONAGE_FILM]* shocks herself by saving the man she was sent to kill.

## 5. Subtract Purpose
- Radical characters turn their backs on the world. Their meditations meander pointlessly; they find creativity pretentious and self-knowledge painful.
- Stuck between inner and outer life, they try to flee both and live in neither.
- [AGING_MAN] stands with a banana protruding from his face, unable to bite down (*[AGING_MAN]'s Last Tape*).
- Trapped characters: in an incomplete play (*[METATHEATRICAL_PLAY]*), in mindless repetition (*[ABSURDIST_PLAY]*), in bureaucracy (*[KAFKAESQUE_NOVEL]*), in [CLASSICAL_PLAYWRIGHT]'s play (*[MINOR_CHARACTER_A] and [MINOR_CHARACTER_B]*).
- The perfect metaphor for today's loss of purpose is the zombie.

## 6. Subtract Unity
- A complex character is one person acting in two directions. Unstable shards of a radical role fragment the character.
- Participatory theatre splinters roles into character and performer (*[IMMERSIVE_PLAY]*, *[IMMERSIVE_PLAY]*).
- Objects become characters that compound themselves (*[LITERARY_WORK]*).

## 7. Subtract Maturity
- The mature mind mediates between the world and her instincts, negotiating inner peace.
- The radical character is an eternal child who surrenders to the world (obedience) or to impulses (violence) or both.
- [CHILDLIKE_MAN] in *[SURREAL_NOVEL]* is childlike, ruminating at the bottom of a well.
- [SURREAL_SCREENWRITER]'s screenplays show little interest in maturity: a psychologist teaches table manners to mice (*[FILM_WORK]*).

## 8. Subtract Conscience
- Evil characters commit unregretted acts because they have no conscience. Radical evil goes further.
- [RADICAL_THINKER]: "Through cruelty one rises to heights of superhuman awareness."
- Radical evil is Sadism: unutterable pleasure and transcendent purity in the act of torture itself.
- [DARK_LORD] is not radically evil—he fights for power but isn't sadistic. [THOUGHT_POLICE] in *[DYSTOPIAN_NOVEL]*, content in his pleasures, watching rats devour [DISSIDENT_PROTAGONIST]'s face—that is radical evil.

## 9. Subtract Belief
- Ask: How crazy is my character? Sane/sane, insane/sane, sane/insane, or insane/insane?
- Conventional characters trust that the world makes sense. [AMORAL_CON_MAN] (insane in a sane world) and [WONDERLAND_TRAVELER] (sane in an insane world) are conventional because they still believe in meaning.
- Radical characters lack any such belief. They cannot find meaning in outer or inner life.
- [SENSORY_OBSESSIVE] in *[HISTORICAL_NOVEL]*, void of belief, entices a starving mob to cannibalize him—the ultimate escape from absurdity.
