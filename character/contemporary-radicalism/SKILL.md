# Contemporary Radicalism

The postmodern avant-garde died decades ago and was replaced by a retro-garde. Today's leading edge reverses both classical satire and [ABSURDIST_PLAYWRIGHT]'s absurdism by placing radical characters in conventional settings, then drawing us into empathy despite our better natures.

## The Historical Progression
1. **Classical satire** (Swift): clear-minded narrators ridiculed lunatic societies.
2. **[ABSURDIST_PLAYWRIGHT]'s absurdism**: radical characters in absurd settings ridiculed reality itself.
3. **Contemporary radicalism**: radical characters in conventional settings—the most disturbing combination, because empathy has nowhere to hide.

## The Realism/Nonrealism/Radicalism Triangle
- **Realism**: conventional characters in conventional worlds—characters with the powers they have in ordinary life.
- **Nonrealism**: conventional characters in unconventional worlds—characters with powers far beyond the ordinary (supernatural, magical, futuristic).
- **Radicalism**: unconventional characters in unconventional worlds—warped powers, fragmented selves, meaningless settings.
- All three extremes meet and overlap, creating infinite character possibilities.
- The fully expressed character built around unified dimensionality is standard in both Realism and Nonrealism. These inhabit the hits and best sellers.

## The Empathy Question
- As writers push the limits of empathy, they ask readers and audiences to identify with increasingly crazed and dangerous characters.
- The answer to "How dark is too dark for the contemporary reader/audience?" seems further and further away.
- The more a writer loses faith in meaning, the more she feels a pull toward Radicalism.

## Application
- Consider placing radical characters inside realistic settings rather than absurd ones—this creates maximum tension between empathy and revulsion.
- Think carefully about your audience's tolerance: Realism and Nonrealism satisfy most readers. Radicalism alienates many but captivates a devoted few.
- Use the subtraction techniques (see nine-subtraction-techniques) to calibrate exactly how far toward the radical extreme to push.
