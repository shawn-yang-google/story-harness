# Values and Subtext in Performance

Design how characters reveal their true selves through performance strategies and first impressions.

### Text vs. Subtext
- **Dual Awareness**: Create a gap between outward sayings/doings and inward thinking/feeling.
- **The Symphonic Mind**: Imply an orchestra of unspoken thoughts beneath a few spoken notes.
- **Show vs. Tell**: Dramatize surface behaviors to imply inner life rather than explaining it directly.

### Storytelling Strategies
- **Mystery**: Put characters ahead of the audience. Use secrets to provoke curiosity.
- **Suspense**: Keep the audience and cast in the same moment of ignorance regarding the future.
- **Dramatic Irony**: Put the audience ahead of the characters. Focus on "how and why" rather than "what."

### Free Will vs. Fate
- **Prospective Free Will**: Ensure choices seem open as they happen.
- **Retrospective Fate**: Make the ending feel inevitable, as if character psychology and social forces left no other choice.

### The Power of First Impressions
- **The Entrance**: Delay the protagonist's arrival to build intrigue. Use an impactful "entrance" that introduces their core self.
- **Framing**: Frame major characters with actions that reveal their temperament (e.g., playing chess against oneself).
- **Impact**: Ensure the first encounter invites insight and sets the trajectory for the character's future.
