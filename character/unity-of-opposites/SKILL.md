# Unity of Opposites

The philosopher [ANCIENT_PHILOSOPHER] argued that reality holds itself together through contradiction: hot/cold merge to create temperature, birth/death forge life. This principle applies to character. Within the harmony of every well-designed role, a set of living contradictions intersect. The unity of opposites is the founding principle of character complexity.

## Four Properties of a Dimension
1. **Consistency**: A dimension must recur. A one-time kindness is a gratuitous gesture, not a dimension.
2. **Variability**: The contradiction must swing between poles depending on relationships—not the same event repeating identically.
3. **Unpredictability**: From moment to moment, who knows which side will show up? The pattern must progress, not repeat.
4. **Unresolvability**: The tension must seem impossible to resolve. An atheist who subconsciously yearns for the eternal; a neuroscientist who freely chooses to believe free will is an illusion.

## Case Study: [EPIC_HERO] (8 Dimensions)
- Pragmatic/Idealistic, Obedient/Defiant, Truthful/Deceitful, Brilliant/Blundering, Protecting/Jeopardizing, Honorable/Thieving, Cool/Raging, Faithful/Unfaithful.
- What makes [EPIC_HERO] endure is the **dark side** of his dimensions: impulsive, hard-hearted, raging, thieving, self-serving, deceitful traits that expand his character beyond any clichéd hero.
- Defining dimension: Brilliant/Blundering. He devised the Trojan Horse yet blundered into the cyclops's cave.

## Case Study: [MOB_PROTAGONIST] (12 Dimensions)
1. Guilt-ridden/Sociopath — the [AMBITIOUS_THANE]-like axle of his inner nature
2. Proud/Shamed — proud of his son's gentleness, shamed by the boy's weakness
3. Calculating/Intuitive — months of detective work, then murders based on a dream
4. Depressed/Optimistic — despair versus hope at war within
5. Loyal/Treacherous — devoted to his wife, betrays her with mistresses
6. Calm/Panicked — cool under pressure yet a victim of panic attacks
7. Fearless/Terrified — fearless before gangsters, terrified by terrorism
8. Sneering/Envying — sneers at normal lives yet envies them
9. Kills/Saves — kills humans, protects animals
10. Loves women/Fears women — macho sex addict tortured by castration nightmares
11. Loves family/Hates family — loves and hates mother, uncle, and above all himself
12. Loves life/Loves death — enjoys beauty but finds death increasingly intoxicating

- [MOB_PROTAGONIST] is far more dimensional than [TRAGIC_PRINCE]. His dimensions crisscross all levels of being across 86 episodes. Yet he cannot change. The series asks: will [MOB_PROTAGONIST] become morally better? Answer: No.

## Application
- Build dimensions that are consistent, variable, unpredictable, and unresolvable.
- The dark side of a dimension is what creates depth—any clichéd hero can act out positive charges.
- Use dimensions to cross all three planes: characterization, true character, and subconscious.
