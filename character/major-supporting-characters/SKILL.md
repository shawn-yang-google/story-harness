# Major Supporting Characters

Design first-circle characters that orbit the protagonist, each possessing their own desires while serving the central narrative.

### Support Roles

- **Change Events**: Ensure major support characters actively turn the course of the story, unlike service roles.
- **Round Out Dimensions**: Give these characters multiple traits that suggest a life outside the enacted events.
- **Subplot Protagonists**: Use support characters to drive intersecting plots that affect the central arc.

### Specific Functions

- **Focal Character**: Use a supporting role to draw exceptional energy or interest if the story requires decentering the protagonist.
- **Foil Character**: Back the protagonist with a reflective "foil" to:
    - Illuminate their traits through contrast.
    - Provide a voice of reason or see what the protagonist cannot.
    - Contradict or challenge the protagonist's morality.
    - Guide the audience toward a full understanding of the central figure.
    - Interpret the protagonist's epiphany or complexities.
- **POV Character**: Utilize a first-person supporting narrator (like [LOYAL_COMPANION]) to filter the story of a mysterious protagonist.
- **Antagonists**: Directly oppose the protagonist’s struggle to restore life's balance.
