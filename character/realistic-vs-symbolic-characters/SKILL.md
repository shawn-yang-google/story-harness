# Realistic vs. Symbolic Characters

Distinguish between characters rooted in fact (Realism) and those rooted in abstraction (Symbolism).

### Ten Key Differences
1. **Truth**: Contrast Realism's factuality with Symbolism's wish fulfillment.
2. **Character**: Focus on complex psychology (Realist) versus imposing personality (Symbolic).
3. **Conflict**: Target inner demons (Realist) versus outer antagonistic forces (Symbolic).
4. **Complexity**: Build multidimensional contradictions versus solid, trait-bound symbols.
5. **Detail**: Provide dense, particularized details versus essential, simplified traits.
6. **Comprehension**: Reward concentration on mystery versus instant recognition of types.
7. **Worldview**: Maintain a skeptical, tough-minded stance versus a sentimental one.
8. **Endings**: Resolve with ironic duality versus simple, pure moral outcomes.
9. **Dynamics**: Reveal inner change and flexibility versus rigid, unchanging natures.
10. **Social Power**: Portray fluid class dynamics versus rigid, static hierarchies.

### Application Principles
- **Types**: Use specific behaviors to elevate an extra into a recognizable type.
- **Stocks**: Assign social roles and jobs for instant recognition of purpose.
- **Stereotypes**: Avoid reducing archetypes to prejudice or biased fallacies.
- **Balance**: Mix qualities from both poles to create unique, believable inhabitants.
