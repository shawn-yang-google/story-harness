Explore the tension between extrinsic (social) and intrinsic (biological) forces to deepen character motivations. Analyze how historical and modern perspectives shape your understanding of human nature.

### Master Extrinsic Perspectives
*   **Social Determinism:** Apply [SOCIAL_THEORIST]’s view that social forces determine consciousness and that identity is a continuous transformation.
*   **Cultural Conditioning:** Use Critical Theory to see the mind as an organ shaped by social environments and subjective cultural beliefs.
*   **Archetypal Roles:** Adopt Campbell’s theory that characters can be cast as roles within universal monomyths passed through cultures.

### Master Intrinsic Perspectives
*   **Subconscious Commands:** Apply [PSYCHOLOGIST]’s model of id, ego, and superego to identify which internal component drives the character.
*   **Biological Computation:** Use Cognitive Science to view the mind as a biological computer designed by evolution with decipherable codes.
*   **Identity Paradox:** Explore William James's view of the self as a singular identity coexisting with a "stream of consciousness" of former selves.

### Integrate Randomness and Coincidence
*   **Account for Luck:** Recognize that randomness often determines a life’s direction before nature or nurture take effect.
*   **Collision of Forces:** Factor in the "haphazard collisions" of worldly forces—family impulses, weather, and chance encounters—that trigger character reactions.
*   **Reaction to Chance:** Design character behaviors as specific reactions to coincidental events where nature and nurture finally intersect.
