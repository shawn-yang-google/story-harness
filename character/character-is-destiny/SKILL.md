# Character Is Destiny

An invisible force shapes a character's life, but it emanates from within himself. As [ANCIENT_PHILOSOPHER] put it: "Character is destiny." The choices we make under pressure shape our future.

### The Destiny Pattern

1. Based on his beliefs, a character's core self determines his choices and actions; conversely, his choices express his true character.
2. His choices under pressure shape his future — revealing who he is and how he changes into who he becomes.
3. The more various the antagonisms in his life, the more diverse his choices.
4. The more diverse his choices, the more complex his nature — more dimensional, unpredictable, surprising, and revealing.
5. His character becomes only as multidimensional as the story's complex of antagonistic forces.

### From Belief to Action

- As a young character comes of age, external forces affect his sense of values: What is worth pursuing? What's foolish?
- When the subconscious reinforces inclinations, they become beliefs. These convictions, rooted in true character, guide all subsequent choices.
- Picture two ships in a raging storm: one stays afloat, the other goes under. The difference is not luck but who captains.

### Application

- Design escalating levels of antagonism — physical, social, personal, inner — to multiply dimensionality.
- Make the character's inner world the ultimate battleground where destiny is forged.
