# Empathy vs. Sympathy

Calibrate the audience's emotional connection through discovery and recognition.

### Seven Performance Functions
1. **Intellectual Pleasure**: Spark insight through unique traits and surprising behavior.
2. **Develop Empathy**: Create a shared humanity to prevent indifference.
3. **Drive Suspense**: Merge curiosity about nature with concern for well-being.
4. **Psychological Puzzle**: Use contradictory desires to create inner suspense.
5. **Surprise**: Reveal character through unpredictable but in-character reactions.
6. **Illuminate Others**: Use characters to develop the dimensions of the rest of the cast.
7. **Insight**: Encourage readers to explore human nature through the character's journey.

### Empathy (Identification)
- **As If**: Engage the "hypothetical gear" where the mind experiences trial emotions.
- **Center of Good**: Anchor identification in a positive core that reflects the audience's self-perception.
- **Instinct**: Trigger spontaneous identification with a "someone like me" quality.

### Sympathy (Likability)
- **Likability**: Foster external feelings for "pleasant" or "agreeable" characters.
- **Distance**: Maintain an outside-in view rather than a vicarious inner experience.

### Balancing Acts
- **Balance of Good**: Position the protagonist as the "good bad guy" relative to their world.
- **Underdog Status**: Cast the protagonist against enormous odds to draw empathy.
- **Attitudes**: Use the cast's reactions to signal how the audience should feel.
