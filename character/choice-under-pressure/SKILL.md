# Choice Under Pressure

A human being is the choices he makes over a lifetime. True character reveals itself not through what a character says but through what he does when the stakes are highest.

### The First Law of Choice

- Nature compels every living thing to act toward the positive as it sees the positive. Clear choices between negative and positive are trivial — the core self always chooses life.
- The only dramatically compelling decision is a choice between two things of more or less equal value.

### Two Types of Dilemma

- **Positive Dilemma**: Two equally desirable but irreconcilable possibilities. He wants both but must choose one.
- **Negative Dilemma**: Two equally undesirable possibilities. He wants neither but must choose one evil.

### Three Grand Principles

1. When a character at risk and under pressure pursues desire, his choices of action express his true character.
2. The greater the risk and pressure, the deeper and truer the choice.
3. The freer the choice, the even deeper and truer it is to the character's identity.

### Degrees of Freedom

- [JEALOUS_GENERAL]'s jealousy blinds him; [AGING_KING]'s daughters decide for him; witches funnel [AMBITIOUS_THANE]. But [TRAGIC_PRINCE]'s choices are freer and more numerous — kill himself or not, revenge or not, love Ophelia or not — making him [CLASSICAL_PLAYWRIGHT]'s most complex character.
- If a character makes few risk-free choices around a single value, he stays shallow. If he makes numerous risk-taking choices across various values, he becomes complex and deeply involving.

### Application

- Build escalating pressure through antagonism. The greater the risk, the more revealing the choice.
- Design dilemmas, not clear choices. Let characters struggle between equally weighted options.
