# The Character Triangle

Map character possibilities across the conflicting treatments of reality.

### The Triangle Model
1. **Realism**: Limit characters to the powers and constraints of ordinary life. Focus on unified selves, moral balance, and rational perceptions.
2. **Nonrealism**: Grant powers far beyond the ordinary. Place conventional characters in unconventional worlds (supernatural, magical, futuristic).
3. **Radicalism**: Warp the powers of the first two extremes to challenge the limits of characterization.

### Design Principles
- **Realism**: Use offhanded underplay and close observation to create characters who believe they live in our world.
- **Nonrealism**: Use grand metaphors and wish fulfillment to express idealized or bizarre realities.
- **Integration**: Overlap these corners to create an infinite variety of roles.
- **Consistency**: Ensure characters believe in their worlds, regardless of how bizarre the rules of causality are.
- **Metaphor**: Utilize nonrealistic settings to externalize internal psychological states.

### Action
- **Choose the Corner**: Determine whether the story requires ordinary limits or extraordinary extensions.
- **Apply Rules**: Define the laws of physics and causality for the chosen reality.
- **Focus**: Maintain conventional character beliefs even within unconventional settings.
