# English Counterexamples

## Definition

A COUNTEREXAMPLE for an invalid sequent is an English argument with the same logical form, whose premises are all *obviously* true and whose conclusion is *obviously* false.

## Requirements

1. **Same logical form** as the invalid sequent
2. Premises must be **obviously true** (public knowledge)
3. Conclusion must be **obviously false** (public knowledge)
4. Do NOT use personal information or hypothetical scenarios

## Example

Invalid sequent: `P → Q, Q ⊢ P`

Counterexample:
- If Los Angeles is in Canada, then Los Angeles is in North America. (T)
- Los Angeles is in North America. (T)
- Therefore, Los Angeles is in Canada. (F)

## Bad Counterexamples

**Hypothetical** (not obviously true/false):
- "If it is raining then there are clouds. There are clouds. Therefore, it is raining."
- Problem: You may not know if there ARE clouds right now.

**[CLASSIC_FILM]l info** (not general knowledge):
- "If my cousin is intelligent, she will pass logic..."
- Problem: No one knows about your cousin.

## Strategy

Use well-known geographic, scientific, or mathematical facts where truth/falsity is uncontroversial.
