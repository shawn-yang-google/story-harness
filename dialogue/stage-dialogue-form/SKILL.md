# Stage Dialogue Form

Theatre exists in a symbolic space defined by the "As If" convention. The audience signs a contract to suspend disbelief, allowing the playwright to use language that transcends naturalism.

## Actionable Principles

*   **Embrace the Poetic License:** On stage, characters can speak in metaphors, tropes, and heightened rhythms that would feel false on film. The stage is a world of voice.
*   **The "As If" Convention:** Treat the stage not as a literal room, but as a space where the internal becomes external. Language is the primary tool for world-building.
*   **Musicality of Speech:** In theatre, dialogue often functions like music. The rhythm, tempo, and tone carry as much weight as the literal meaning.

*   **Musical Theatre:** The ultimate extension of stage dialogue where the emotion becomes so heightened that speech naturally transitions into song. Songs *are* dialogue.

## Practical Guidance

*   **Scale Up:** If the dialogue feels too mundane, look for opportunities to heighten the imagery. Use similes and metaphors that express character essence.
*   **Rhythmic Focus:** Read the lines aloud. Are they staccato and aggressive, or flowing and lyrical? Match the rhythm to the dramatic tension.
*   **Voice over Image:** Remember that while a camera shows, the stage *tells* through the character's perspective. The audience sees what the character describes.
*   **Acknowledge the Audience:** Whether through direct address or simply the projection required for the space, stage dialogue is always an act of public "overhearing."
