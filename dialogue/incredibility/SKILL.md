# Incredibility

Incredibility occurs when dialogue feels hollow or phony. Writers often try to fix this with "naturalism" (adding "um," "ah," and stuttering), but naturalism is rarely the cure.

## Actionable Principles

*   **Dialogue ≠ Conversation:** Conversation is dilutive and messy. Dialogue is concentrated and purposeful. Adding "um" and "uh" just makes your dialogue messy, not credible.
*   **Credibility vs. Actuality:** Credibility is about *fictional authenticity*. Does this character, in this world, with this motivation, say this thing?
*   **The Internal Logic Test:** A character in a surrealist play can speak in riddles and be perfectly credible if they are true to their internal logic.

## Causes of Incredibility

1.  **Exposition on the Nose:** Characters telling each other things they both already know for the audience's benefit.
2.  **Consistency Failure:** A character suddenly using words or rhythms that don't match their established background or personality.
3.  **Lack of Subtext:** Characters saying exactly what they feel (being "on the nose").

## Practical Guidance

*   **Gauge Against Authenticity:** Don't ask "Would a person say this?" Ask "Would *this* character say this *now*?"
*   **Subtext Search:** If a line feels fake, try saying the opposite or making the character lie. Often, the truth is what makes a line feel "written."
*   **Check the Rhythm:** Phony dialogue often has a repetitive, sing-song rhythm. Break the pattern.
*   **The "Why Now?" Test:** If a character suddenly reveals a secret, is there a dramatic necessity for it, or is the author just pushing the plot? Forced revelations lead to incredibility.
