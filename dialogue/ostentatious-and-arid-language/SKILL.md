# Ostentatious and Arid Language

Dialogue fails when the "hand of the author" becomes visible. This happens at two extremes: when the writing is too "showy" (ostentatious) or when it is too "dry" (arid).

## 1. Ostentatious Language: The Literary Swagger
Ostentatious dialogue is self-conscious. It values the "beauty" of the sentence over the reality of the character. It breaks the bond of belief by reminding the reader they are reading a book.

### Actionable Principles:
- **The "Authorless" Ideal:** Aim for dialogue that feels like it arose naturally from the character's soul, not the author's thesaurus.
- **Kill Your Darlings:** If a line is so "clever" that it makes the reader stop to admire the phrasing, it’s probably pulling them out of the story.
- **Prioritize Truth over Beauty:** A messy, broken sentence that reveals character is superior to a polished one that masks it.

## 2. Arid Language: The Desert of Polysyllables
Arid dialogue is dry, Latinate, and clinical. It feels like a legal brief or a technical manual rather than human speech.

### Actionable Principles:
- **Avoid the Abstract:** Replace "We must facilitate an exit strategy" with "We need to run."
- **Inject Vitality:** Use concrete nouns and active verbs. Avoid the "passive-arid" voice ("It was decided that the meeting should end").

## Key Example:
- **Ostentatious:** "The incandescent orb of the sun descended, a bloody coin slipping into the slot of the horizon, much like my hope."
- **Arid:** "The meteorological conditions were such that visibility was reduced to a suboptimal level."
- **Balanced:** "The sun went down. I couldn't see a damn thing."

## Practical Guidance:
Check the balance of your technique. If the reader notices the *writing* instead of the *feeling*, the dialogue has failed. Aim for the "transparent" quality where the words disappear and only the character's struggle remains.
