# Prose Dialogue Form

Prose offers a unique advantage: the ability to move seamlessly between external speech and internal thought. It balances non-character narration with the distinct "voice" of the inhabitants.

## Actionable Principles

*   **The Spectrum of Voice:** Prose can range from strictly objective "he said/she said" scenes to pure stream of consciousness.
*   **Free Indirect Style:** The narrator's voice adopts the idioms and attitudes of the character without explicitly using "he thought." This blurs the line between narration and dialogue.
*   **Interior vs. Exterior:** Use interior dialogue to contradict or complicate exterior speech.

## Six In-Character Tactics

1.  **Direct Dialogue:** Standard quoted speech.
2.  **Indirect Dialogue:** Summarized speech ("He told her he was leaving").
3.  **Free Indirect Speech:** Narrated speech in the character's voice.
4.  **Internal Monologue:** Direct access to thoughts (First-person).
5.  **Stream of Consciousness:** Unfiltered, often chaotic flow of thought/perception.
6.  **Mental Dialogue:** Character arguing with themselves or an imagined other.

## Practical Guidance

*   **Tag Judiciously:** Avoid "he ejaculated" or "she pondered." Stick to "said" or use action beats to attribute speech.
*   **Voice Consistency:** Ensure the character's internal voice matches the rhythm and vocabulary of their external speech, or use the discrepancy as a character point.
*   **The Pivot:** Use a transition from external dialogue to internal thought to show a character's true reaction to a lie or an insult.
