# Last Words

Dialogue is verbal action — the writer's foremost vehicle for expressing character, advancing story, and creating the transparency through which readers and audiences glimpse the truth of human nature.

## The Essential Truth
Every line a character speaks performs an action. Every action serves a desire. Every desire connects to the deepest needs of a human being struggling through life. When these connections are strong, dialogue lives. When they break, dialogue dies.

## What Mastery Requires
- **Relentless practice:** Write draft after draft. No line arrives perfect. The best writers are the most ruthless rewriters.
- **Honest self-criticism:** Develop the taste to distinguish a true phrase from a clever one. When in doubt, tone it down.
- **Deep empathy:** The Magic If — imagining yourself as each character — is not a technique but a way of being. You must feel what they feel before you can write what they say.
- **Patience:** Voice is not a choice; it's a result. Your distinctive style emerges from years of talent wrestling with content.

## The Hierarchy of Craft
1. Know your story (event design, character design, desire, antagonism)
2. Know your characters (psychology, culture, vocabulary, personality)
3. Know your scene (intention, turning point, beats of action/reaction)
4. Then, and only then, write the dialogue

Dialogue is the frosting atop layers and layers of subtext. Without the layers beneath, the frosting has nothing to cling to.

## Final Principle
Dialogue problems are story problems. When scenes fail, the fault is rarely in the words. The solution lies deep within event and character design. Fix the story, and the dialogue will follow.

The art of dialogue is never finished — only abandoned. Keep writing.
