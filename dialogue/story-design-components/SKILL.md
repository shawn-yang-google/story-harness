# Story Design Components

Dialogue is the "skin" of a story. If the skeleton underneath (the story design) is broken, the skin will sag. Before writing a single line, ensure these components are functional.

### The Foundation of a Scene

1. **Inciting Incident**
   - The event that upsets the balance of the character's life and kicks off a desire.
   - **Check:** Does this scene have a reason to exist? What was the "inciting incident" for this specific moment?

2. **Story Values**
   - Every story moves between positive and negative binaries (Life/Death, Love/Hate, Freedom/Slavery).
   - **Check:** What value is at stake in this scene? If no value changes, the dialogue is just "talk."

3. **The Desire Complex**
   - **Object of Desire:** What the character wants.
   - **Super-Intention:** The life-long goal.
   - **Motivation:** Why they want it.
   - **Scene Intention:** The specific goal *now*.
   - **Background Desires:** Subconscious needs.
   - **Actionable:** A character without a desire is a character who has nothing to say.

4. **Forces of Antagonism**
   - Conflict must come from one of four levels: Physical (the world), Social (institutions), [CLASSIC_FILM]l (others), or Inner (the self).
   - **Check:** What is stopping the character from getting what they want *right now*?

### Progression and the Spine

- **Spine of Action:** The "through-line" that connects all scenes.
- **Progression:** Each scene must top the previous one in intensity or revelation.
- **Actionable:** If the dialogue feels repetitive, it’s likely because the story isn't progressing. You're circling the same conflict instead of escalating it.

### Summary
Dialogue is the final expression of story design. If the dialogue is failing, go back to the Desire Complex and the Forces of Antagonism.
