# Line Design

How you structure a sentence determines its dramatic or comic impact. By controlling the placement of the "core word" (the noun or verb that provides meaning), you control the audience's tension.

## The Three Sentence Designs

1.  **Periodic (Suspense) Sentence:** Withholds the core word until the very end. This builds curiosity and tension. It is the most dramatic and comic design.
    *   *Example:* "After all the lies, the theft, and the betrayal, I still found myself wanting to... stay."
2.  **Cumulative Sentence:** Puts the core word first, then develops it with modifiers. This feels natural and conversational.
    *   *Example:* "I'm staying, despite the lies, the theft, and the betrayal."
3.  **Balanced Sentence:** Puts the core word in the middle, surrounded by subordinate clauses. This creates a sense of stability or intellectual control.
    *   *Example:* "While I know you lied, I will stay, because I have nowhere else to go."

## Actionable Principles

*   **The Suspense Sentence Power:** Use periodic sentences for punchlines or emotional revelations.
*   **Stichomythia:** Rapid-fire, one-line exchanges. It creates a sense of verbal combat or intense shared emotion.
*   **Mixed Designs:** Real speech is a messy mix of these designs. Use cumulative for exposition and periodic for "the turn."

## Key Examples

*   **[CRIME_ANTHOLOGY_SERIES] (Rust Cohle):** Cohle's nihilistic speeches often use mixed designs—starting with a cumulative observation about the world and ending with a periodic "suspense" word that hammers home his point.
*   **Classic Comedy:** The setup is often a cumulative sentence; the punchline is almost always a periodic sentence where the "funny word" comes last.

## Practical Guidance

*   **Identify the Core:** Look at your most important lines. Where is the "meaning" word? If it's in the middle, try moving it to the end to increase impact.
*   **Vary Sentence Length:** Monotony kills dialogue. Mix short, staccato lines with longer, more complex designs.
*   **The Punchline Rule:** Never put a word *after* the laugh or the shock. End the sentence on the hit.
